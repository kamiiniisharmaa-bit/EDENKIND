The story behind
the curious cabinet

✦ Founded in the spirit of wonder, rooted in ethical craft ✦
“We believe objects carry memory — and the most meaningful ones are made slowly, by hand, with respect for earth and artisan.”

EDENKIND began as a small notebook of sketches and a radical idea: what if commerce could feel like stepping into a cabinet of curiosities — where every piece tells a story, every purchase supports a maker, and beauty is never divorced from ethics?

Founded in 2022 by curator and textile anthropologist Mara Wren, the studio grew from a nomadic pop-up into a global haven for uncommon objects. We traverse remote villages, forgotten ateliers, and urban workshops to find artifacts that defy the ordinary — from hand-carved ceremonial masks to naturally dyed ikat weavings.

More than a marketplace

EDENKIND is a collective of storytellers, archivists, and artisans. We don't just sell things — we preserve traditions, celebrate imperfections (wabi-sabi), and champion a slower, more intentional way of acquiring beauty. Every object in our collection has a provenance, a maker's name, and a carbon-conscious journey to your home.

✦ Our pillars ✦

Principles that guide every object, every relationship

Radical Transparency

Full origin stories, material sourcing, and pricing breakdowns — no greenwashing, no hidden supply chains.

Circular Ethos

Carbon-neutral shipping, reclaimed packaging, and a repair-before-replace philosophy.

Artisan First

Fair wages, direct partnerships, and profit-sharing with every community we work with.

Slow Curation

We release limited batches, not endless collections — each piece is given time and attention.

The EDENKIND pledge

We reject fast trends, throwaway culture, and anonymous production. Every object in our collection has been chosen with reverence — for the hands that made it, the materials that formed it, and the home that will hold it. 1% of every sale is donated to the Indigenous Artisan Fund. We are not perfect, but we are always learning.
