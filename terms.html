2. Objects & authenticity

Every piece offered at EDENKIND — from handwoven textiles to sculptural oddities — is either sourced directly from independent artisans, recovered from ethical salvage, or produced in small batches in our partnered ateliers. We guarantee each object’s description: materials, provenance, and artisan attribution are detailed with honesty. For vintage or reclaimed items, natural signs of age (patina, slight irregularities) are part of the object’s soul and not considered defects.

All measurements, colors, and imagery are provided in good faith; however, variations in lighting or screen resolution may cause slight differences. If you ever question authenticity, our curator team is available to discuss — we stand behind our sourcing.

Slow Promise: Every object listed on EDENKIND is either carbon-neutral shipped or part of a reforestation contribution.

4. Shipping & rituals

We aim to ship in-stock items within 3–5 business days. For fragile or ceremonial objects, additional packing care may extend handling up to 7 days. Risk of loss or damage passes to you upon delivery; however, if a package arrives visibly damaged, please notify us within 48 hours so we can file a claim on your behalf.

5. Returns & the wabi-sabi clause

We offer returns within 14 days of delivery for eligible items (excluding final sale, perishable, or intimate goods). To initiate a return, contact our curatorial team. Items must be returned in the same condition as received, with original wrapping where possible. Refunds are issued to the original payment method minus a 5% restocking fee that supports our artisan network.

Non-returnable items: custom commissions, archived one-of-a-kind pieces, and objects marked “patina preserved” (natural wear is celebrated). If you simply changed your mind about a unique artifact, we encourage you to gift it rather than return — but we’ll still honor the 14-day window with empathy.

The Wabi-Sabi Clause: Because each object has an organic history, we do not accept returns based on minor variations in grain, texture, or handmade irregularities — these are marks of authenticity.
